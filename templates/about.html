Intelligent Portfolio Optimization

This web application helps you optimize your investment portfolio based on Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT), developed by Nobel Prize winner Harry Markowitz. By uploading a CSV file of asset prices or returns, you can find the ideal asset weights to achieve specific investment goals while managing risk effectively.

Key Features

Powerful tools for professional portfolio management

Simply upload your historical price or return data in CSV format. Supports daily, weekly, and monthly frequencies.

Advanced Optimization

Uses powerful numerical optimization algorithms (SciPy) to find mathematically optimal portfolio allocations.

Rich Visualizations

Interactive efficient frontier plots and portfolio weight charts help you understand your investment strategy.

Optimization Objectives

Choose from three powerful optimization strategies

Maximize Sharpe Ratio

Finds the portfolio with the best risk-adjusted returns. This strategy balances return maximization with risk minimization, ideal for most investors.

Best for: General investing, retirement portfolios, balanced risk approach
Minimize Variance

Creates the lowest-risk portfolio possible with your selected assets. Prioritizes capital preservation over high returns.

Best for: Conservative investors, capital preservation, risk-averse strategies
Target Return

Achieves a specific return level with minimum risk. You set your desired return, and the optimizer finds the safest way to reach it.

Best for: Goal-based investing, specific return requirements, institutional mandates

Understanding Your Results

Efficient Frontier Chart

The Efficient Frontier shows the set of optimal portfolios that offer the highest possible expected return for each level of risk. Points on this curve represent the most efficient combinations of your assets.

  • X-axis: Portfolio volatility (risk)
  • Y-axis: Expected annual return
  • Red dot: Your optimized portfolio
Portfolio Weights Chart

The Weights Chart displays the percentage allocation of each asset in your optimized portfolio. These weights represent how much of your total investment should go into each asset.

  • Positive weights: Long positions (buy and hold)
  • Negative weights: Short positions (if enabled)
  • Total: All weights sum to 100%

Mathematical Foundation

Modern Portfolio Theory is built on rigorous mathematical optimization. Here are the key formulas driving your results:

Portfolio Return

E[Rp] = Σ (wi × E[Ri])

Expected portfolio return equals the weighted sum of individual asset returns
Portfolio Risk (Variance)

σp² = Σ Σ (wi × wj × σij)

Portfolio variance considers both individual asset risks and correlations between assets
Sharpe Ratio

SR = (E[Rp] - Rf) / σp

Risk-adjusted return measure: excess return per unit of risk
